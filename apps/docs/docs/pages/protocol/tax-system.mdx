# Tax System

0xSlots uses **Harberger taxation** (Partial Common Ownership) as its core economic mechanism.

## How It Works

1. **Self-assessed price** — The occupant declares the value of their slot
2. **Continuous tax** — A percentage of that price accrues continuously to the slot's `recipient`
3. **Deposit escrow** — The occupant pre-funds a deposit that gets drawn down as tax accrues
4. **Force-buy** — Anyone can purchase the slot at the occupant's stated price

### Tax Rate

The `taxPercentage` is expressed in **basis points per month**:

| Basis Points | Monthly Rate | Annual Rate |
|---|---|---|
| 100 | 1% | ~12% |
| 500 | 5% | ~60% |
| 1000 | 10% | ~120% |

### Tax Calculation

Tax accrues linearly based on the self-assessed price:

```
taxOwed = price × taxPercentage × elapsedSeconds / (30 days × 10000)
```

Tax is settled (deducted from deposit) on every state-changing operation: `buy`, `release`, `selfAssess`, `topUp`, `withdraw`, `liquidate`.

## Deposit Escrow

The occupant maintains a deposit that covers future tax:

- **Top up** — `topUp(amount)` adds to the deposit
- **Withdraw** — `withdraw(amount)` removes from deposit (subject to `minDepositSeconds`)
- **Auto-deduction** — Tax is deducted from deposit during settlement

The `minDepositSeconds` parameter (set at deployment) ensures occupants maintain enough deposit to cover a minimum period of tax.

## Liquidation

When the deposit is fully depleted by accrued tax, the slot becomes **insolvent**. Anyone can call `liquidate()`:

1. Remaining deposit goes to cover owed tax
2. Slot returns to vacant state (occupant = zero address, price = 0)
3. Liquidator receives a bounty (`liquidationBountyBps` of the owed tax)
4. Pending updates (tax, module) are applied

:::tip
Liquidation is a permissionless economic incentive. Bots and keepers can monitor slots and earn bounties by liquidating insolvent occupants.
:::

## Tax Collection

Accumulated tax sits in `collectedTax` until someone calls `collect()`:

- **Permissionless** — anyone can trigger collection
- Tax is sent to the slot's `recipient` address
- Protocol fee (if enabled) is sent to the factory fee recipient

## Mutable Tax

If `mutableTax` is `true` at deployment, the `manager` can propose tax rate changes:

1. Manager calls `proposeTaxUpdate(newRate)`
2. Update is stored as pending — does **not** take effect immediately
3. Update applies on the next **ownership transition** (`buy`, `release`, `liquidate`)
4. Manager can cancel with `cancelPendingUpdates()` before it applies

This ensures the current occupant's terms are **never changed under them**. The new rate is visible before any transition, so prospective buyers can see the pending change.
